Composite Performance Index Scoring Formula

Weighted Scoring System

Performance Index Score =
  (Weighted Brand Health Growth × 30%) +
  (Barrier Improvement Rate × 25%) +
  (Ad Diagnostics Win Rate × 25%) +
  (Media Effectiveness × 20%)

Brand Health Weighting: Awareness (25%) + Consideration (35%) + Intent (40%)
Media Effectiveness: Brand health points per billion impressions (CPM-neutral)
Normalization: Proportional scoring where each dimension sums to 100 across markets

How to Read This Table

Weighted Growth: Calculated using Awareness (25%) + Consideration (35%) + Intent (40%) to reflect conversion proximity.
Improvement Rate: Percentage of tracked barriers that showed improvement (e.g., 4 of 6 = 67%).
Win Rate: Percentage of ad diagnostics metrics where celebrity campaign beat non-celebrity benchmark.
Media Effectiveness: Brand health points generated per billion impressions - higher scores indicate more efficient conversion of reach into brand impact.

Key Insight

Creative Quality Trumps Media Cost

USA leads media effectiveness with 18.39 brand health points per billion impressions - 47% more efficient than France (12.54) - despite paying 2.19x higher CPM ($10.02 vs $4.57).

This validates a critical principle: Creative execution quality is more valuable than media cost advantages. France's low CPM couldn't compensate for weak ad diagnostics (33% win rate, 60% appeal, 19% association).

Strategic Implication: Invest in creative excellence over volume. USA delivered superior performance through quality impressions, not quantity.
