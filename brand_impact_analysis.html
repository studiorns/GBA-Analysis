Strategic Achievement

The celebrity campaign broke an 18-month decline trajectory (Q4'23-Q3'24), recovering consideration to 44.7% and intent to 27.8%, matching Q3'23 pre-crisis consideration baseline. This represents the first sustained brand metric improvement since the geopolitical crisis, with celebrity campaign achieving +0.9pp higher consideration lift vs non-celebrity campaigns in UK and France markets.

Advertising Effectiveness: Seen vs Not Seen Analysis

Methodology Note

"Lift" measures the difference in brand metrics (consideration or intent) between respondents who have seen Abu Dhabi advertising versus those who have not. Across all markets and campaign periods, those exposed to advertising consistently showed higher consideration and intent than those not exposed, with positive lift ranging from +0.7pp to +11pp. For example, if 44% of those who saw ads would consider Abu Dhabi vs. 38% who didn't, the lift is +6pp. Campaign averages compare: Non-Celebrity Period (Q3'23-Q3'24, five quarters) vs. Celebrity Campaign (Q4'24-Q1'25, two quarters). The difference between these averages shows the relative advertising effectiveness improvement.

Critical Challenge: Safety Perception

Safety perception worsened in 3 of 4 markets during the celebrity campaign, with significant increases in USA (+5.8pp), Germany (+5.1pp), and France (+2.7pp). This likely reflects ongoing external geopolitical factors rather than campaign failure, as the celebrity campaign coincided with continued regional developments. Safety messaging should be reinforced through complementary channels and destination reputation management strategies.

Learnings for Future Celebrity Campaigns

1. UK: Sustain Dominance Strategy

Performance Data

  • Best overall performer: 67% improvement rate (4 of 6 barriers)
  • Ad diagnostics dominance: 66% recall, 37% association, 87% appeal
  • Highest consideration lift: +7.5pp (celebrity vs non-celebrity: +0.9pp advantage)
  • Only market winning all 3 ad diagnostic categories

Learnings for Future Campaigns

  • No major strategy changes needed - current approach validates celebrity effectiveness
  • Continue investment at current levels; UK demonstrates highest ROI across all metrics

2. USA: Capitalize on Growth Momentum

Performance Data

  • Highest total growth: 4.8 points (awareness +1.9pp, consideration +2.0pp, intent +0.9pp)
  • Strongest ad appeal: 93% (highest across all markets and campaigns)
  • Children entertainment barrier: -4.0pp improvement (strongest performer)
  • Consideration lift: +5.5pp average during celebrity period

Learnings for Future Campaigns

  • Amplify family-focused content - children entertainment barrier shows proven success (-4.0pp)
  • Maintain balanced awareness + consideration messaging that drove +1.9pp and +2.0pp growth
  • Leverage 93% ad appeal advantage through sustained celebrity visibility
  • Priority: Address safety perception worsening (+5.8pp) through PR coordination

3. Germany: Fix Awareness-to-Consideration Gap

Performance Data

  • Awareness success: +1.8pp (tied for highest growth)
  • Consideration failure: +0.5pp (lowest across all markets)
  • Conversion disconnect: 3.6x gap between awareness and consideration growth
  • Ad effectiveness: Equal performance (5.0pp lift in both periods)
  • Barrier performance: 50% improvement (3 of 6)

Learnings for Future Campaigns

  • Problem diagnosis: Awareness is working (+1.8pp); consideration conversion is not (+0.5pp)
  • Test message variants emphasizing rational benefits and specific experiences
  • Address authenticity concern (+2.1pp worsening) with destination credibility content
  • Monitor intent lift (65 on radar vs 50 baseline) as conversion indicator
  • Do not increase awareness investment; focus on mid-funnel optimization

4. France: Maintain Steady Progress

Performance Data

  • 2nd highest total growth: 3.5 points (awareness +0.8pp, consideration +1.1pp, intent +1.6pp)
  • Highest intent lift focus: +1.6pp (best conversion performance)
  • Ad recall: 59% (only diagnostic category where France exceeded non-celebrity baselines)
  • Consideration lift advantage: +0.9pp celebrity vs non-celebrity campaigns
  • Barrier performance: 50% improvement (3 of 6)

Learnings for Future Campaigns

  • Address top-funnel creative execution gaps - France shows lowest ad appeal (60%) and brand association (19%) across all markets, indicating celebrity creative isn't resonating despite strong bottom-funnel performance
  • Test localized creative variants with French cultural references and complementary French influencer partnerships to improve ad diagnostics while maintaining celebrity presence
  • Preserve bottom-funnel momentum - strong intent growth (+1.6pp) validates current conversion messaging
  • Address children entertainment concern (+4.3pp worsening) with family-focused content featuring destination experiences

5. Critical Cross-Market Priority: Safety Perception

Factual Performance Data

  • USA: +5.8pp worsening (17.0% to 22.8%)
  • Germany: +5.1pp worsening (18.6% to 23.7%)
  • France: +2.7pp worsening (15.1% to 17.8%)
  • UK: -0.4pp improvement (only market showing progress)

Learnings for Future Campaigns

Reality: Safety perception is beyond marketing control and reflects external geopolitical developments during Q4'24-Q1'25. Celebrity campaign could not address this barrier through paid advertising alone.

Learning: Future safety perception challenges would require coordination with DCT government communications and PR teams for earned media strategy, sustained messaging through official channels, travel industry partnerships, and third-party validation. Marketing efforts showed greatest success when focused on controllable barriers: value (-1.1pp USA), authenticity (-2.0pp USA), knowledge (-1.7pp UK).

Bottom Line

Campaign Validation

Factual conclusion: Celebrity campaign period coincided with reversal of 18-month brand decline (+2.5pp consideration recovery), achieved 67% ad diagnostics win rate (8 of 12 metrics), and improved 54% of barriers (13 of 24). English-speaking markets (UK 67% barrier improvement, USA 4.8 total growth points) demonstrate strongest performance, suggesting cultural and linguistic affinity with Chris Hemsworth drives superior resonance. Germany requires mid-funnel optimization. France requires creative execution improvements to address lowest-in-market ad appeal (60%) and brand association (19%) while preserving strong bottom-funnel momentum. Safety perception requires cross-functional response beyond marketing scope.
